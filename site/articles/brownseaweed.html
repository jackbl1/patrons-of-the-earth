Brown Seaweed: The Climate's Favorite Superfood


Brown Seaweed, currently just a minor inconvenience on your everyday beach walk, has the potential to be a major player in the fight against climate change. Not just major player: this food could be the holy grail of a climate friendly diet. Brown seaweed grows up to 50cm per day, one of the fastest-growing plants in the world, and so removes carbon from the environment at an unparalleled rate. Brown seaweed also offers an impressive array of health benefits. For the purpose of avoiding such an unappealing name for such an appealing dietary staple, I will be referring to brown seaweed by a nickname that I just came up with on the spot, Ocean Lettuce.

It’s widely known that the meat industry is one of the most damaging industries on the environment. On top of that, farms for other food products have significant environmental impacts as well. Take almonds for example. It takes approximately 15 gallons of water to produce just 16 almonds.[1] Taking into account the energy usage in producing and distributing that much clean water, on top of the land and energy usage in the farming, almonds have a nontrivial impact on the environment.

Enter Ocean Lettuce. Ocean lettuce comes in two varieties, Fucus vesiculosus (also known as bladderwrack) and Laminaria japonica. Both are carbon negative crops, meaning carbon dioxide is removed from the environment when the whole farming process is said and done, and both can grow easily in sectioned areas of natural bodies of water. You can even grow seaweed in your own house!

Now, let’s talk diet. Just because something is easy to grow doesn’t mean that it is easy to eat. Why would I eat something that looks like the trail a slug leaves behind after sliming its way across my window. Well, it’s actually great for you. Brown seaweed has been seen to enhance immune response, enable weight loss, and improve gut microbiome in rats.[2] This food contains a healthy amount of carbohydrates, dietary fibers, and prebiotics. Put some ocean lettuce on your burger (preferably with an impossible meat patty), make a seaweed salad, blend seaweed into your salad dressing, toss sauteed seaweed into your omelette, bake your seaweed into seaweed chips. The opportunities are endless.
